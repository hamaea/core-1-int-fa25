leaves in autumn!

when i was younger, i'd love to jump in the leaves in our front yard after my dad raked everything onto the curb.

this was until i learned about ticks in dry leaves.

step 1

start with the colored side facing up and fold it in half diagonally.

step 2

fold along the dotted lines to create the leaf stem (you can adjust the width).

step 3

bring down the top corner and fold along the dotted line (strip size can vary).

step 4

bring the corner up and fold the paper to align with the bottom edge of the lower layer.

step 5

again, fold the corner down to align with the top edge of the lower layer.

step 6

again, fold the corner up to align with the top edge of the lower layer.

step 7

repeat folding the model up and down until you reach the top part of the paper.

step 8

stretch the model by unfolding the pleated folds you just made.

step 9

fold the top layer along the dotted line at the bottom right corner.

step 10

repeat the same fold on the other side.

step 11

lift open the top layer to complete the shape of the leaf.
