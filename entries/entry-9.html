let's make a star

what kind should we make?

i always made these in elementary school. in both japanese school and my day-to-day american school, both were made by different cultures but for the same purpose. throwing.

step 1

white side up. fold each sheet in half vertically, then unfold (you just want the center crease).

step 2

on both sheets, fold the left + right edges into the center line.

step 3

fold both papers in half lengthwise so they become long rectangles.

step 4

fold the corners in along the guide lines so the edges line up clean. the two pieces should be mirror images.

step 5

fold the top corners down, then fold the bottom corners up so the shape looks like a clean zig-zag / “S” unit.

step 6

flip one unit over. rotate the other unit 45°.

step 7

stack them: place the rotated unit on top of the flipped unit.

step 8

fold along the lines and tuck the lower tips into the upper pockets (lock it in).

step 9

turn the whole model over.

step 10

repeat the same locking move: fold and tuck the remaining tips into the pockets.

fin.

ok now you can throw it.
